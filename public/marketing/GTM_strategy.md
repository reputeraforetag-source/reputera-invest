## Risk Mitigation & Contingency Plans

### Risk 1: CAC Increases Beyond 500 SEK
**Mitigation:**
- Diversified channel strategy (not dependent on one channel)
- Build owned channels (SEO, partnerships) with lower marginal CAC
- Referral program reduces blended CAC over time
- Continuous A/B testing and optimization

**Contingency:**
- Slow customer acquisition pace
- Increase ARPU through premium features
- Focus on higher LTV segments (annual plans)

### Risk 2: Slower Adoption in Craftsmen Segment
**Mitigation:**
- Multiple sub-segments within craftsmen (electricians, plumbers, etc.)
- Geographic diversification (if Stockholm slow, focus Gothenburg)
- Accelerate Phase 2 if Phase 1 slower than expected

**Contingency:**
- Move to Phase 2 (healthcare) earlier (Month 12 instead of 19)
- Pilot additional verticals (restaurants, salons)

### Risk 3: Competitive Response
**Mitigation:**
- First-mover advantage in Swedish craftsmen vertical
- Superior product (Smart Routingâ„¢, AI quality) creates switching costs
- Lock in customers with annual contracts
- Build trade association relationships (exclusive partnerships)

**Contingency:**
- Accelerate product development (stay ahead on features)
- Increase marketing spend to capture market faster
- Strategic partnerships for distribution

### Risk 4: International Expansion Challenges
**Mitigation:**
- Phase 3 delayed until Phase 1-2 proven
- Local country managers (not Stockholm-based)
- Light localization strategy (Norwegian/Danish similar to Swedish)
- Partnership-heavy approach (reduce direct costs)

**Contingency:**
- Focus on Sweden Phase 2 expansion (more verticals)
- Delay Norway/Denmark to Year 4
- Explore Finnish market (different language but less competition)
