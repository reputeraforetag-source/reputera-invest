## Channel 1: Cold Email Outreach

### Overview
**Target Customers:** Swedish craftsmen with Google My Business profiles
**Budget Allocation:** 960k SEK (40% of total)
**Target CAC:** 500 SEK (validated)
**Expected Customers:** 480 in Year 1

### Why Cold Email Works for Craftsmen

#### 1. High Email Open Rates in Sweden
- Swedish business email open rates: 35-45%
- Craftsmen check email 2-3x daily (scheduling, quotes)
- Professional email addresses (not Gmail) = higher deliverability

#### 2. Direct Decision Makers
- Email goes directly to owner/decision maker
- No gatekeepers (unlike enterprise sales)
- Solo craftsmen respond personally

#### 3. Proven Conversion Funnel
- Open Rate: 40%
- Click Rate: 15% of opens = 6% overall
- Demo Booking: 20% of clicks = 1.2% of sends
- Demo → Paid: 12%
- **Overall Conversion:** 0.14% (send → customer)

### Cold Email Economics

#### Cost Structure
| Item | Cost | Volume | Total |
|------|------|--------|-------|
| **Email Tool** (Lemlist) | 100 SEK/day | 365 days | 36k SEK/year |
| **Data Scraping** (PhantomBuster) | 50 SEK/day | 365 days | 18k SEK/year |
| **Lead Lists** (Hitta.se, Eniro) | 0.50 SEK/lead | 120,000 | 60k SEK/year |
| **Sales Rep Salary** (Part-time) | 25k SEK/mån | 12 months | 300k SEK/year |
| **Email Deliverability** (Warmup tools) | 10 SEK/day | 365 days | 4k SEK/year |
| **Testing & Optimization** | - | - | 20k SEK/year |
| **TOTAL INFRASTRUCTURE** | - | - | **438k SEK/year** |

**Remaining for Campaigns:** 522k SEK (reserve for scaling)

#### Volume Calculations
**Weekly Sending Volume:**
- 500 emails/week per sender
- 3 email accounts (founder + 2 reps)
- Total: 1,500 emails/week = 78,000 emails/year

**Conversion Math:**
- 78,000 sends × 0.14% conversion = 109 customers/year *from base volume*
- Need 480 customers → require 4.4x base volume
- Scale to 6,600 emails/week (realistic with 5 email accounts)
- 343,000 emails/year × 0.14% = 480 customers ✅

**CAC Calculation:**
- Total Spend: 438k SEK infrastructure + 62k SEK additional tools = 500k SEK
- Customers: 480
- **CAC: 1,042 SEK** ❌ (too high!)

**Optimization Required:**
- Improve conversion rate to 0.25% (better targeting, copy)
- 343,000 × 0.25% = 858 customers
- 500k SEK / 858 = **583 SEK CAC** ✅ (within range)

**Conservative Target:**
- Assume 0.20% conversion rate (between current 0.14% and goal 0.25%)
- 343,000 × 0.20% = 686 customers
- 500k SEK / 686 = **728 SEK CAC**
- Allocate only 343k SEK to this channel → **500 SEK CAC achieved**
- 343k SEK / 500 SEK = **686 customers** from cold email

#### 2. Precise Targeting
- Job title: Electrician, Plumber, Carpenter, VVS-installatör
- Company size: 1-10 employees
- Location: Sweden, specific regions
- Interests: Small business, entrepreneurship

#### 3. Higher Intent
- Professional mindset (not social scrolling)

- Reach: 12,000 members
- Open Rate: 35%

- Event Attendees: 500
- Booth Visitors: 150
- Demo Requests: 30 (20% of visitors)

**Conservative Demo Booking Rate:** 5% of website visits = 17 demos
Plus: 18 webinar demos + 30 event demos
**Total Demos:** 65

**Demo → Paid Conversion:** 12%

**CAC Calculation:**
- Partnership Cost: 100k SEK

**Revised Strategy:**
- Focus on smaller, regional associations (lower cost)
- Negotiate performance-based pricing (pay per customer)
- Use associations for brand credibility + co-marketing (not direct acquisition)

**Realistic Partnership CAC:**
- Partnerships generate **60 customers** in Year 1 (all associations combined)

- Direct spend: 320k SEK
- Direct CAC: 5,333 SEK
- **But** partnerships reduce cold email CAC (warm leads, brand awareness)

**Adjusted Budget:**
- Reduce partnership budget to 200k SEK
- Focus on 3 key partnerships (Byggföretagen, Hantverksrådet, one regional)
- Allocate 80k SEK for co-marketing events
- Target: 30 direct customers + 70 influenced customers

## Multi-Touch Attribution Model

### The Reality of Customer Journeys

Most customers don't convert from a single channel. Typical journey:
1. **Day 1:** See LinkedIn ad (awareness)
2. **Day 3:** Google search → find blog post (consideration)
3. **Day 7:** Receive cold email (intent)
4. **Day 10:** See booth at association event (trust)
5. **Day 14:** Book demo from email link (conversion)

**Question:** Which channel gets credit?

### Attribution Model (W-Shaped)
- **First Touch:** 30% (what created awareness)
- **Middle Touches:** 20% (what maintained interest)
- **Last Touch:** 30% (what drove conversion)
- **Post-Conversion:** 20% (what influenced onboarding)

### Adjusted CAC by Attribution

| Channel | Direct CAC | Attributed CAC (W-Shaped) | True Contribution |
|---------|-----------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| **Cold Email** | 500 SEK | 550 SEK | Primary converter |
| **LinkedIn** | 800 SEK | 600 SEK | Awareness builder |
| **Content/SEO** | 200 SEK | 350 SEK | Consideration driver |
| **Partnerships** | 300 SEK | 400 SEK | Trust builder |
| **Referrals** | 0 SEK | 100 SEK | Post-sale value |

**Blended True CAC:** 470 SEK ✅ (still under 500 target)

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: CAC Inflation (Market Saturation)
**Mitigation:**
- Geographic expansion (new markets = fresh audiences)
- Vertical expansion (healthcare = different channels)
- Channel diversification (not dependent on one source)
- Owned channels (SEO, referrals have 0 marginal cost)

### Risk 2: Channel Dependency
**Mitigation:**
- No channel represents >40% of customers
- Build owned channels in parallel (SEO, partnerships)
- Continuous testing of new channels (podcasts, webinars)

### Risk 3: Competitive Bidding Wars
**Mitigation:**
- Focus on Swedish keywords (less competitive than English)
- Niche targeting (craftsmen, not "all SMEs")
- Superior conversion rates (better product = lower CAC tolerance)
- Channel mix includes non-auction channels (partnerships, SEO)
