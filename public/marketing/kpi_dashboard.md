#### CAC Payback Period

**Definition:** Months to recover customer acquisition cost from gross margin

**Formula:** CAC / (Monthly ARPU √ó Gross Margin %)

**Calculation:**
- CAC: 1,000 SEK (blended target)
- Monthly ARPU: 400 SEK
- Gross Margin: 81%
- **Payback Period:** 1,000 / (400 √ó 0.81) = **3.1 months**

**Target:** <6 months (industry benchmark)
**Current:** 1.5 months (500 SEK CAC, 400 SEK ARPU)
**Status:** üü¢ Excellent (industry-leading efficiency)

#### LTV:CAC Ratio

**Definition:** Customer Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost

**Calculation:**
- LTV: 129,600 SEK (from [metrics dashboard](../../progress/metrics-dashboard.md))
- CAC: 500 SEK
- **LTV:CAC:** 259:1

**Target:** >200:1 (exceptional for SaaS)
**Current:** 259:1
**Status:** üü¢ Exceptional

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- 1:1 = Losing money on customers
- 3:1 = Healthy but slow growth
- 5:1 = Excellent efficiency

- 200:1+ = **Unicorn territory** (us!)

### Attribution Model

**Multi-Touch Attribution (W-Shaped):**
- **First Touch:** 30% (what created awareness)
- **Middle Touches:** 20% (what nurtured consideration)
- **Last Touch:** 30% (what drove conversion)
- **Post-Conversion:** 20% (what influenced retention)

**Example Customer Journey:**
1. Saw LinkedIn ad (first touch) ‚Äì 30% credit to LinkedIn
2. Read 3 blog posts (middle touches) ‚Äì 20% credit to content
3. Received cold email, booked demo (last touch) ‚Äì 30% credit to email
4. Association webinar influenced final decision (post-conversion) ‚Äì 20% credit to partnership

**Why W-Shaped:**
- More accurate than last-touch (gives content/partnerships credit)
- More actionable than linear (emphasizes key touchpoints)
- Aligns with multi-channel strategy
